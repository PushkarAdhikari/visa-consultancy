World-renowned universities, shorter degree programs, and post-study work opportunities up to 3 years.

Many students go to countries like Canada, Australia, etc. But the UK is also one of the best options for those who want to study abroad. Unlike before, the UK now allows work permits for those students who complete their graduation from UK colleges and institutions. The work permit can go up to 3 years after completing the studies in the UK.

Why Study in the UK?

UK qualifications are recognized all over the world. Graduates return home to successful careers globally.

Graduates from the UK are preferred by major corporations of the world.

International students have become important world influencers — Imran Khan, Mahatma Gandhi, Dr. Manmohan Singh, Bill Clinton, and more.

Ideal location to explore Europe. Best-in-class roads, air, and rail links allow economic and hassle-free travel.

Shorter duration programs — most undergraduate honors take 3 years, Master's takes 1-2 years, saving on tuition & accommodation.

Student identity cards offer numerous discounts on transport and sightseeing across Europe.

UK at a Glance

  • Capital: London
  • Currency: Pound Sterling (£)
  • Language: English
  • Population: ~67 Million
  • Top Universities: Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, UCL, LSE
  • Bachelor's: 3 years | Master's: 1-2 years
  • Post-study Work: Up to 3 years
  • Visa Type: Tier 4 (Student) Visa

Living in the UK

Even though the expense of transport, sightseeing, etc. cost comparatively less, there are some initial costs which will need careful planning, especially if you plan to stay in the UK longer (i.e. students, workers, etc.). Here are the essential things you should budget for:

Budget Essentials

  • Accommodation: Major expense in London. Hostels from £11/night
  • Transport: Oyster card recommended for commuting convenience
  • Clothing: Pack for all seasons; formal wear for interviews
  • Bond & Rent: Up to 6 weeks rent as bond; £800-1000/month typical
  • Eating & Drinking: Home cooking is much more economical than eating out
  • Travel: Student discounts available on rail and sightseeing

London is not cheap in terms of Breakfast, Lunch, or Dinner. It is recommended to buy groceries and make your meals at home which will surely be much more economical. Save these bucks on important things like traveling or doing your hobbies.
