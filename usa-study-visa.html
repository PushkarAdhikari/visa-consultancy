USA Study Visa

World's most popular destination for higher education. Unmatched flexibility, technology, and opportunities.

The United States of America is the number one and most popular destination among international students searching for higher education abroad. The universities of America are well known for their teaching and research quality.

For international students in the world, the USA has the most flexible and versatile education system. Currently, there are almost 30% of the total international students in the world studying in the USA.

Why Study in the USA?

Advanced Technology — US universities are leaders in scientific techniques and technology, committed to offering the latest technical resources to students.

Campus Life — Variety of academic, athletic and cultural activities make students socially active. Many have cricket teams, fraternities, clubs.

Global Education — US degrees are recognized internationally. Builds decision-making abilities, self-confidence, and long-term career success.

Flexibility — Many programs and courses with the opportunity to specialize in multiple fields. Students can alter programs to meet specific career goals.

USA at a Glance

  • Capital: Washington, D.C.
  • Currency: US Dollar ($)
  • Language: English
  • Population: ~331 Million
  • Top Universities: Harvard, MIT, Stanford, Yale, Princeton
  • Visa Type: F-1 Student Visa
  • Work: CPT during study + OPT up to 3 years (STEM)
  • Intakes: Fall (Aug) & Spring (Jan)

Living in the USA

America is no. 1 in having international student population with almost 60,000 students from the world to experience life and education in the USA. Almost 4% of the students are international ones at a higher level of education and the numbers are still growing rapidly.

American Culture: The hallmark of the USA is independence. Americans value independence — they assume you take care of yourself unless you ask for help. So remember to reach out whenever you need advice or assistance.

In oppose to stereotypes of individuality and independence, the majority of America's population gains its identity by being in groups. Many students join groups to make friends. However, this is not a universal law — like any other country, Americans are not always easy to understand. Keep an open mind and learn about them individually.
