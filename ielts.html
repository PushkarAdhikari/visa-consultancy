IELTS Coaching

Achieve your dream band score with expert guidance. 15+ years of excellence in IELTS preparation.

IELTS which is also known as International English Language Testing System (IELTS) compute the proficiency level in English of a non-native user who needs to use their English skills in an Academic or Professional environment. The exam is also for those who want Visa approval through various countries immigration.

The advantage of IELTS is that it's not a limited seats exam. Anyone who qualifies the four parameters of English Language will be able to achieve good band score. You will have score as much possible out of 9 bands. The band varies from 1 = bad user to 9 = excellent user.

IELTS contain two kind of exams which is IELTS Academic and IELTS General exam. Both contain the four major aspects of English Language i.e. Listening, Reading, Writing and Speaking. The only difference between these two is only the Writing module.

Four Modules of IELTS

Master each module with our structured coaching approach. Here's what each section covers:

Listening

This module is considered the easiest of the four. Duration is around 30 minutes. You'll be given headphones to listen to specific conversations or recordings. The accent can vary from any country. There will be around 40 questions including Fill in the Blanks, Maps, etc.

Reading

Candidates are given 3 different articles or paragraphs with various types of questions totaling 40. Duration is 1 hour. This test assesses skimming, fast reading, author's mindset and feeling. Questions can be True/False/Not Given, Fill in the Blanks, Name the appropriate title, etc.

Writing

Assesses sentence making, vocabulary, preposition, paraphrasing and grammatical aspects. Two tasks: Task 1 requires writing at least 150 words on a report (Maps, Table, Pie charts, etc.), and Task 2 requires writing at least 250 words on a given statement. Total duration is 1 hour.

Speaking

Evaluates pronunciation, vocabulary, fluency, and other parameters in a one-on-one conversation. Divided into 3 parts: introductory questions, cue card section (speak on a topic for 1-3 minutes), and follow-up questions. Duration is 11-15 minutes.
