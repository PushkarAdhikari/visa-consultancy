New Zealand Study Visa

British-based education system, stunning natural beauty, and a safe, welcoming environment for students.

In the department of international education, New Zealand has made a big name for itself. The country is a wonderful place to live as it provides from excellent quality of education to an unmatched standard of living.

With a strict assurance quality system, the education regime is controlled which offers you the flexibility to choose the institute of your choice along with the town or city based on your interest.

The country has a wide range of education systems with a total of 8 universities, 16 institutes of Polytechnic and Technology, and plenty of private institutes and colleges, all of which are known for their best-in-class education.

Why Study in New Zealand?

Education programs and degrees are based on the world's most accredited system — the British system.

Gained an international reputation as one of the best education providers.

Progressive education system with numerous state of the art facilities.

Safe learning environment with excellent opportunities for international students.

Excellent adventure playground for thrill-seeking sports along with relaxation and serene places.

Tuition fees are cheap compared to USA, UK, Australia, or Canada.

New Zealand at a Glance

  • Capital: Wellington
  • Currency: New Zealand Dollar (NZD)
  • Languages: English, Māori
  • Population: ~5 Million
  • Universities: 8 + 16 Polytechnics
  • Education System: British-based
  • Work: 20 hrs/week during study
  • Post-study Work: Up to 3 years

Employment & Work Rights

New immigration regulations introduced in 2005 allow students to work while studying and post-study work opportunities:

  • Eligible candidates can work up to 20 hours per week during studies (previously 15 hours)
  • Students studying a 12-month course or above are eligible for full-time work during summer holidays
  • Graduates from 2-3 year diploma, degree, or post-graduation are eligible for a 6-month Job Search Permit
  • After finding a job related to their qualification, the work permit extends to the validity of their job offer

About New Zealand

New Zealand is a stunning country situated in the southern part of the Pacific Ocean. The country is created by two islands and has a surface area roughly the size of Great Britain, California, or Japan. The cities, culture and sceneries are diverse and vibrant.

New Zealand is home to around 5 million multi-cultural population including people from the Pacific islands and several parts of India, Africa, Asia, Europe, and the Middle East. 14 percent of the total population is Indigenous Maori. The country represents many major religions free from discrimination, ensured by Human Rights Legislation.

Climate

Located in the southern hemisphere, January and February are the warmest months. Autumn is March to May, winter is June to August, and spring is September to November. The climate is temperate with mild, warm dry summers and wet winters.
