Singapore Study Visa

A perfect blend of Eastern culture and Western business. World-class education in Asia's leading financial hub.

Singapore is one of the dynamic cities which are rich in color and contrast. It's a country where you will find a perfect combination of food, culture, arts and infrastructure. This City State has been successfully able to mix Eastern culture and Western business.

There is the world's largest oil refining center located in Singapore and the country is at 4th position as a leading financial center. Even the World Bank has noted that Singapore is the easiest place in the world to do business. The world's best institutions are attracted to Singapore to set up their international campuses due to the country's strong reputation for high-quality education.

Why Study in Singapore?

Singapore is considered an excellent education hub with its best-in-class universities and institutes providing quality education at an affordable price. In 2013 QS Best Student Cities, Singapore won 3rd place — representing the combination of diverse student communities with highly ranked institutions and living standards.

Singapore has many private and public institutions that meet international education standards for students from all over the world.

Universities rank among the top 100 in the whole world. Excellent academic reputation globally.

Students are provided unique learning methods along with sophisticated modern living that is vibrant and cosmopolitan.

Quality education at an affordable price compared to Western countries. Great value for international students.

Singapore at a Glance

  • Capital: Singapore (City State)
  • Currency: Singapore Dollar (SGD)
  • Languages: English, Mandarin, Malay, Tamil
  • Population: ~5.7 Million
  • Top Universities: NUS, NTU, SMU
  • Business: World's easiest place to do business (World Bank)
  • Climate: Tropical (22-34°C year-round)
  • Visa Type: Student Pass

Living in Singapore

Singapore offers one of the best standards of living. According to the Mercer cost of living survey, Singapore is among the top 10 expensive cities of the world. However, with proper planning, you can manage your budget effectively:

Accommodation: Property costs can be high whether renting or buying. Planning ahead is essential.

Food & Groceries: Quite affordable compared to other expenses. Dining in shopping mall food courts and local stalls is very cheap while helping you socialize with diverse people.

Language: English is the most common language used for trade, business and education. Most institutes conduct classes in English. Mandarin, Malay and Tamil are also commonly spoken.

Climate

Singapore enjoys a tropical climate without any sharply divided seasons as the country lies almost on the equator. Temperature generally ranges from 22 to 34 degrees Celsius. Humidity increases with rainfall which can reach 100%. The hottest months are June and July, while monsoon season is from November to December.
